Angkor Wat

Angkor Wat, the largest religious monument in the world, was built in the early 12th century by King Suryavarman II as his state temple and a mausoleum. Originally dedicated to the Hindu god Vishnu, the massive stone complex was later converted to Theravada Buddhism, which remains practiced there today.
